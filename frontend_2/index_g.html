Most Common Diseases

Early Bright

Early blight is a fungal disease caused by “Alternaria solani”, affecting primarily tomatoes and potatoes. It thrives in warm, humid conditions and starts with dark brown or black spots with concentric rings on lower, older leaves. As the disease progresses, affected leaves turn yellow, wither, and drop off, reducing the plant’s health. It can also cause lesions on stems and fruit, further impacting crop yield. The disease spreads through wind, water, and contaminated tools. Management includes crop rotation, resistant plant varieties, and the use of fungicides.

Late Blight

Late blight is a devastating plant disease caused by the pathogen Phytophthora infestans, primarily affecting potatoes and tomatoes. It thrives in cool, moist conditions and spreads rapidly through wind and water. Symptoms include water-soaked, dark lesions on leaves, stems, and fruit, leading to rapid decay and plant collapse. The disease is notorious for causing the Irish Potato Famine. Management includes using resistant varieties, applying fungicides, and removing infected plants. If not controlled, late blight can destroy entire crops in a short time.
